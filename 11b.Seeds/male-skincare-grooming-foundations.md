# Male skincare, grooming & makeup foundations

**Claim:** Skincare is not product shopping — it's understanding your skin's biology first, diagnosing what it needs, then treating specific problems with the simplest routine that works.

## Phase 1 — Understand my skin

Learn skin type (oily, dry, combination, sensitive), the acid mantle, how sebum production works, what causes acne, and why exercise sweat triggers breakouts. Foundation: everything downstream depends on correctly reading what my skin is telling me.

## Phase 2 — Treat pressing problems

Oil control, acne (zits), and sweat-induced breakouts during exercise. Specific ingredients to research: salicylic acid (oil + acne), niacinamide (sebum regulation), benzoyl peroxide (spot treatment). Order of attack: identify triggers → pick one active at a time → observe reaction → adjust.

## Phase 3 — Minimal skincare routine

Build from cleanser + moisturizer + SPF (morning), cleanser + treatment + moisturizer (night). No 12-step nonsense — only what addresses the problems found in phases 1–2.

## Phase 4 — Light makeup for blemishes

Concealer for spot coverage, tinted moisturizer for even tone. Goal: enhance, not cover — the "you but clearer skin" look. Shade matching first, technique second.

## Phase 5 — Full body

Extend skincare logic beyond the face: body wash, body moisturizer, sun protection for exposed areas, and any body-specific acne/ingrown hair concerns.

## Phase 6 — Upgrade routine (budget + time permitting)

Add layers at your own pace: serums (vitamin C for brightness, hyaluronic acid for hydration), eye cream, sheet masks, weekly exfoliation, gua sha or facial massage. No pressure — this phase is optional and comes after the basics are solid.

## Phase 7 — Cosplay makeup skills

Beyond basic concealing: color correction (covering beard shadow, dark circles), foundation matching for full-coverage looks, contouring for face shape, setting techniques for long wear (primers, setting sprays), and specialty products (stage makeup, prosthetics basics). Doors open for creative expression once the fundamentals are comfortable.
