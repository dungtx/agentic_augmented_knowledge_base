## Claim
> For hot humid nights in Vietnam, the challenge isn't just heat — it's navigating three forces at once: ambient heat, AC cold drafts, and open-window wind that hits the nose and throat. The right blanket material handles the first two; protecting the airway requires an additional layer of defense.

## Body
Sleeping in Vietnam's summer means juggling conflicting needs. Some nights you run AC; some nights you open the windows for natural airflow. Both have a hidden cost: AC blasts cold dry air at your body; open windows expose you to rapid temperature drops and random gusts that irritate the nose and throat — especially if you're susceptible to respiratory sensitivity.

**The blanket layer** — for both AC and open-window nights — needs to breathe while buffering. Linen is the top pick: open weave, moisture-wicking, cool-to-touch, and just heavy enough to cut a draft without trapping heat. Cotton gauze/muslin is the lighter, softer alternative. Avoid synthetics (polyester, microfiber, fleece) — they trap heat and don't breathe, making everything worse.

**The airway layer** — for open-window nights where the throat and nose are vulnerable — is separate from the blanket problem:
- A **light scarf or neck gaiter** worn loosely around the neck while sleeping creates a microclimate that warms and humidifies incoming air before it hits the throat
- **Positioning:** don't sleep with the window blowing directly at your face — angle the bed or use a headboard/footboard as a windbreak
- **Humidity:** open windows in VN summer mean dry-season air or rain-season damp. A small bedside humidifier or a wet towel hung near the window can buffer both extremes
- **Nasal protection:** a thin layer of petroleum jelly inside the nostrils before bed helps if nasal passages dry out and become irritated (common with both AC and wind exposure)

**Blanket + airway combined:** linen blanket for body temperature regulation + a light cotton neck wrap for airway protection is the full system for a Vietnam summer night, whether AC or open-window.

### Where to buy in Vietnam
- **Linen blankets/sheets:** Home decor stores in HCMC/Hanoi, H&M Home, Zara Home, Shopee/Lazada. Local Vietnamese linen brands offer good quality at lower prices than imports.
- **Cotton gauze/muslin blankets:** Shopee/Lazada (search "chăn muslin" or "chăn cotton gạc"), baby supply stores.
- **Light scarves/neck gaiters:** Decathlon, Shopee/Lazada, any outdoor or sports store.
- **Bamboo/Tencel:** Limited local availability; Shopee/Lazada or international orders.
