## Situation

I want to run barefoot — preferably a marathon eventually — but I have essentially no experience with barefoot activities. Current goal is to build better running form and stronger feet, not to hit a specific time or distance deadline.

**Current baseline:** cheap leather-based shoes with soft plastic cushion, thin short socks. Can run 2–3 km without trouble. Beyond that: ball of foot hurts, sides of big toes and pinky toes hurt, sore for a day. Likely a mix of connective tissue not yet conditioned + shoes being slightly small/rigid for my feet.

## Key insight

Barefoot running is a fundamentally different gait (forefoot/midfoot strike vs. heel strike). The limiting factor is connective tissue adaptation (feet, ankles, calves), not cardiovascular fitness. Ramping too fast causes injury. The existing foot pain at 2–3 km in cramped shoes suggests my feet need both stronger shoes and deliberate conditioning — barefoot will expose and strengthen those weak points.

A patient, gradual approach with daily short sessions + one long run + recovery is the right structure. The time horizon is open; form and foot strength come first.

## Unknowns

- Terrain availability (grass, track, pavement?) — affects how abrasive barefoot starts feel
- Barefoot vs. minimalist shoe preference for the transition (zero-drop shoes vs. true barefoot)
- What "long run" means at the start (gradual progression from current 2–3 km ceiling)
- Current cadence and strike pattern (heel striker? overstriding?)
- Past injury history (ankle sprains, plantar fasciitis, shin splints?)

## Next actions

1. Research barefoot transition protocols (r/FootFunction, r/BarefootRunning, Born to Run)
2. Build a weekly schedule: 6× ~30-min daily sessions (technique drills + short barefoot jogs) + 1 long run Saturday + recovery activities (foot mobility, calf stretching, toe splay exercises)
3. Start with walking barefoot daily to condition soles — 10–15 min → progress to jogging
4. Track form cues: cadence (180+), light footfall, no heel striking
5. Only scale when connective tissue feels ready, not when cardio says go
6. Consider properly fitting minimalist/zero-drop shoes as an intermediate step
