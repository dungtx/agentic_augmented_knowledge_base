## Claim
> Male skin and hair care isn't complicated — it's a small set of daily habits built on knowing your skin type and climate. In Vietnam's humidity, the wrong product does more harm than no product at all.

## Body

### The minimum viable routine

Three steps, twice a day (morning + night). Everything else is optional.

**1. Cleanse** — wash your face with a cleanser matched to your skin type. Not bar soap (too alkaline, strips oils). Morning: light cleanse or just water if skin is dry. Night: proper cleanse to remove the day's sweat, dirt, and sunscreen.

**2. Moisturize** — even in humid climates. AC and sun both dehydrate skin. A light gel or water-based moisturizer in VN humidity; heavier cream only if you have dry skin or at night.

**3. Sunscreen** — morning only, but non-negotiable in Vietnam. SPF 30 minimum, SPF 50+ ideal. UV index in VN is consistently extreme. Reapply every 2 hours if outdoors. The single highest-impact thing you can do — prevents aging, dark spots, and skin cancer.

### Skin types and what changes in VN humidity

| Skin type | Signs | What to use | What to avoid |
|-----------|-------|-------------|---------------|
| **Oily** | Shiny all over, large pores, acne-prone | Gel cleanser, niacinamide serum, light gel moisturizer, matte sunscreen | Heavy creams, oil-based products |
| **Dry** | Tight, flaky, rough patches | Cream cleanser, hyaluronic acid serum, cream moisturizer, dewy sunscreen | Foaming cleansers, alcohol-based toners |
| **Combination** | Oily T-zone (forehead/nose/chin), dry cheeks | Gentle foaming cleanser, light moisturizer, focus drying products on T-zone | One-product-fits-all approach |
| **Sensitive** | Redness, stinging, reacts to products | Fragrance-free everything, minimal ingredients, physical sunscreen (zinc/titanium) | Fragrance, essential oils, alcohol, harsh actives |

**Vietnam-specific:** humidity makes oily and combination skin more common. If you're unsure, start with products for oily/combination — lighter formulations that won't clog pores in the heat.

### Hair care in VN climate

**The problem:** humidity = frizz, sweat = scalp buildup, hard water in VN = mineral residue, sun = UV damage.

**Shampoo:** every other day, not daily (daily strips natural oils, triggers overproduction). Use a clarifying shampoo once a week if you use product or have hard water buildup.

**Conditioner:** every wash, ends only (not scalp if you have oily hair). Leave-in conditioner or a light hair oil helps with humidity frizz.

**Scalp care:** sun exposure on the scalp (especially if hair is thinning) causes damage. Hat or scalp sunscreen on long outdoor days. Dandruff in humidity is often fungal — ketoconazole shampoo (Nizoral) 2x/week clears it.

**Styling:** water-based pomades and clays over oil-based (oil-based clogs scalp in humidity, harder to wash out). Matte finish reads more natural in casual settings.

**Korean and Japanese drugstore brands** (COSRX, Biore, Anessa, Tsubaki) are widely available in VN via Shopee/Lazada and are well-suited to Asian skin/hair in humid climates. Western brands (Cetaphil, Neutrogena) also available at Guardian/Watson's.
