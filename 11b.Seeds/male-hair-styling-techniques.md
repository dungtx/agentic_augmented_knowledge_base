## Part 1 — Base care for stronger hair & reduce shedding

What strengthens hair: protein treatments (keratin, rice water) for structure, moisture balance to prevent brittleness, scalp health for follicle strength. Reduce fall: identify type (breakage vs shedding from root — different causes, different fixes). Scalp massage + proper washing technique + avoiding over-washing. Diet angle: iron, zinc, biotin, vitamin D — deficiencies show in hair first.

**Open questions for you:** What kind of hair fall are you seeing? Seasonal, stress-related, or constant? Any scalp conditions (dandruff, itchiness, oiliness)?

## Part 2 — Styling products that survive summer heat

VN summer = humidity + sweat + UV. Products melt, hair frizzes, hold drops.

What works: water-based clays and pastes (strong hold, matte finish, wash out cleanly). Avoid oil-based pomades — they melt, attract dust, clog scalp. Pre-style with a blow-dryer on cool setting + sea salt spray for texture that holds longer. Finish with a light hairspray if you need all-day hold.

## Part 3 — Styles that suit you best

This is personal — depends on your face shape, hairline, and daily effort budget. A good stylist + reference photos (what you like) + trying a cut for 2 weeks to see if it works in your routine.

**Finding a barber in Vietnam** — you want someone who understands your hair type and listens, not just someone who gives a standard fade. Look for: barbers/stylists who ask about your routine before cutting, show you the back with a mirror, give styling tips after the cut, and offer consistent quality visit-to-visit. Try 2–3 different places (word-of-mouth in VN / reviews on Google Maps / asking friends with similar hair) and commit to the one that gets you without heavy explanation. Build the relationship — a barber who knows your history will catch issues before you do (receding hairline, thinning patches, scalp conditions).

## Part 5 — Hair dye: considerations & color maintenance

Before dyeing: start with healthy hair (dye on damaged hair = worse damage). Go to a professional for the first time — box dye disasters are costly to fix. Understand your skin undertone (warm vs cool) for which color suits you. Bleach is necessary for light colors and is the most damaging step.

Color maintenance: use sulfate-free shampoo (sulfates strip color faster), wash in cool water (hot opens cuticles, color escapes), reduce wash frequency + use dry shampoo between washes, purple shampoo for blondes/silvers to cancel brassiness, regular deep conditioning (dyed hair is drier), touch-up roots every 4–6 weeks.
