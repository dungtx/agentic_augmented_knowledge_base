# 0001 — Branching interview design with router question

The interview uses a single systems-thinking walkthrough question as a router: candidates who can explain _why_ (architectural reasoning, rejected alternatives, past failures, adaptations) enter Track 1 with deeper systems-probing. Candidates who only describe _what_ (stack, components) enter Track 2 where Critical Thinking becomes the hard gate and differentiation shifts to Adaptability, Proactiveness, and Confidence. This avoids wasting time probing systems thinking on candidates who haven't demonstrated it, while ensuring Track 2 candidates are still evaluated on coachable, high-signal dimensions.

**Status**: accepted

**Considered Options**:
- **Single-track for all candidates**: Would force all candidates through systems-thinking probes they can't answer, burning interview time. Rejected.
- **Pre-screening by CV to determine track**: CVs over-index on past opportunity, not capability. A candidate who never built a distributed system might still reason well about one. Rejected.
- **Branching mid-interview (chosen)**: Tests actual capability, not CV. Adapts to the candidate. Accepted.

**Consequences**:
- Requires interviewer to make a fast routing judgment (10 min) — the threshold is "can they explain why?"
- Requires maintaining two question sets
- Track 2 candidates get a fundamentally different interview; this must be documented so other interviewers don't assume Track 2 means "failed"
