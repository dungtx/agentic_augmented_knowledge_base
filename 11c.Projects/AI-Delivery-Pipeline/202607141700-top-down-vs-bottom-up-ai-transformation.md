# Top-down vs bottom-up AI transformation

## Two approaches

**Top-down:** Build a tool that injects AI into all processes, proving it on one slice (software development delivery). Vision-driven — hinges on the team leader's worldview. Powerful when the vision is right, but fragile: breaks when used by people who don't see the world the same way, and can feel impractical at points.

**Bottom-up:** Build tools aiding day-to-day work, accumulate team knowledge in using AI, then broaden the horizon toward more abstract concepts and pain points discovered through usage. Grounded and practical, but lacks a unifying vision. The well-tested tools can become a double-edged blade — teams settle at a comfortable level and lose momentum to move further.

## Synthesis goal

Want a hybrid: the big vision of a thought leader with the approach of a bottom-up practitioner — someone who knows the real pain points and can build a road to guide people forward. This is about scoping transformation correctly: critique both approaches, extract lessons, so if/when I have the chance to scope things, I can get it right.
