# Step 8 — 90-Day Plan, Goals, and Measurement

Turn the previous 7 steps into an executable operating plan: goals, leading indicators, weekly rhythm, 6-week execution cycles, and a review cadence. Without this, the strategy is theater.

**Mode:** Synthesis. Light interview to calibrate ambition.

**Artifact:** `marketing/08_plan.md`

## What this step produces

A document the user opens every Monday morning to know what to work on. If the final plan is too abstract to answer "what am I doing today?", it's wrong.

## Inputs

- `marketing/01_current_state.md` — for constraints (time, budget, hard-nos)
- `marketing/04_beachhead.md` — for the target + graduation criterion
- `marketing/05_positioning.md` and `marketing/06_messaging.md` — for voice
- `marketing/07_channels.md` — for activities and channel-level metrics

## The planning horizon — 90 days + 6-week cycles

- **90 days** is the *goal horizon*. Beyond 90 days is aspiration, not planning.
- **6-week cycles with 2-week cooldowns** (Basecamp's Shape Up) are the *execution horizon*. Marketing experiments fit this cadence better than calendar quarters.

So: 90-day goals committed at the top, two 6-week cycles with defined work, 2-week cooldowns for review/reset between them.

## Set the 90-day goal (one, maybe two)

Pick **1 goal**, maybe 2. Not three. Not five.

Each goal must be:

- **Specific** — a number, a milestone, a verifiable event
- **Outcome-based, not activity-based** — "50 qualified signups" not "publish 12 blog posts"
- **Leading where possible** — for early stage, revenue is too lagging to drive weekly decisions. Use the North Star metric (delivered value) or a strong proxy.

### North Star examples by business type

From Sean Ellis & Morgan Brown, *Hacking Growth* — pick one metric that represents delivered value:

- SaaS: weekly active teams (not total signups)
- Dev tool: projects using the tool in production / week
- Consulting: qualified discovery calls booked, or active engagements
- Local trade: installed jobs / month (or completed-job count)
- Services: repeat-client bookings or retainer-active clients
- Prosumer / course: weekly active learners or lesson-completion rate

### Goal calibration interview

> "If 90 days from now you looked back and this was the best quarter of marketing work you've ever done, what would have happened? Give me a number or a milestone."

Then pressure-test:

> "Realistic question — given [X hours/week] and [$Y/month], what's the most ambitious version of that you actually believe in?"

If the user has no baseline, set a **range** (conservative / target / stretch) rather than a point. Ellis's rule: "the first goal is learning velocity, not hitting numbers."

### Goal examples by stage

- **Pre-revenue SaaS:** "50 qualified signups; 10 real user interviews; Product Hunt launch completed"
- **Early-revenue consultant:** "3 new clients closed; 5 discovery calls/month by end of Q1; 1 speaking engagement booked"
- **Local trade:** "20 inbound quote requests/month by month 3; 50 Google reviews (up from 12); 2 active referral partnerships with contractors"
- **Creator / prosumer:** "500 new newsletter subscribers; 2 sponsorship conversations; launch first paid offering to waitlist"

### Goals to reject

- "Grow brand awareness" — unmeasurable
- "10× revenue" — unrealistic at most stages
- "Post consistently" — activity, not outcome
- "Have a better strategy" — meaningless

## Map goals to channel activities (work the math)

For each goal, trace the path backward through the inner-ring channel(s) from step 7:

> "To hit 20 inbound quote requests/month at a 5% GBP-view-to-inquiry conversion, we need 400 Google Business Profile views/month. At our current rate of [X], we need [specific actions]."

If the math doesn't work, one of these must change: the goal, the channel, or the activity level. Force this confrontation now. It's better to discover impossibility in planning than in month 2.

## MVP OKR structure (optional, for the user who likes it)

Full OKRs are overkill for a solo operator. Minimum-viable: **1 Objective, 3 Key Results, quarterly.**

- **Objective:** the 90-day goal in one sentence (directional)
- **Key Results:** 3 numeric, outcome-based measures (not activities)

Example:
- O: Land our beachhead with 3 reference customers
- KR1: 3 paying customers closed in the [beachhead] segment
- KR2: All 3 have given at least one named referral
- KR3: Average sales cycle under 14 days

Skip OKRs entirely if the user finds them friction-inducing. Don't impose structure for its own sake.

## Leading indicators per channel

From step 7's kill criteria, expand into full per-channel leading indicators:

| Channel | Leading (weekly signal) | Mid-cycle (3-week signal) | Lagging (90-day) |
|---|---|---|---|
| **SEO / content** | Articles published, pages indexed | Avg position for target cluster | Organic-sourced signups |
| **Paid search/social** | CTR, CPC trend | Landing conversion rate | CAC, LTV:CAC |
| **Partnerships** | # outreach conversations | # partners committed | Partner-sourced leads |
| **Referrals** | Referral asks made | NPS / "would recommend" % | Referred customers closed |
| **Sales outbound** | # personalized touches | Reply rate | Meetings booked → closed |
| **Community hosting** | Posts/week, replies | New engaged members | Community-sourced signups |
| **Local GBP** | Reviews solicited, posts published | Profile views, direction requests | Quote-request inbound |
| **LinkedIn organic** | Posts published | Avg impressions/post | DM-sourced leads |

Pick the leading indicator(s) for your inner-ring channel. Weekly review = leading only. Monthly review = all three layers.

## Review cadence

Lenny Rachitsky's "boring consistency" principle: same day, same template, every week.

| Cadence | What | Length |
|---|---|---|
| **Weekly** (e.g. Friday) | Leading indicators only. One decision, one adjustment. | 30 min |
| **End of 6-week cycle** | All three metric layers. Kill/continue experiments. Re-rank backlog. | 60–90 min |
| **2-week cooldown** | No new work. Review, reset, plan next cycle. | ad hoc |
| **90-day** | Full goal review. Rerun this skill in iteration mode. | half day |

Indie Hackers norm: publish metrics publicly (Pieter Levels, Jon Yongfook) to enforce the cadence. Optional but effective.

## Weekly rhythm — the document the user actually uses

This is the most load-bearing section. Draft a realistic weekly routine matched to the user's stated hours.

Example — solo SaaS founder, 5 hrs/week:

```
Monday (60 min) — Review last week's leading indicators. Pick one content topic.
Tuesday (90 min) — Draft the week's one content piece.
Wednesday (45 min) — Publish + cross-post + send to list.
Thursday (60 min) — Inbound: respond to DMs, book calls.
Friday (45 min) — One outbound action (10 cold emails OR 1 partnership outreach OR 1 Reddit contribution).
```

Example — trades business owner, 3 hrs/week:

```
Weekly, flexible:
- 60 min — Ask last week's completed jobs for Google reviews (phone or text)
- 45 min — Post one before/after photo with caption to GBP + Instagram
- 45 min — Respond to all quote requests within 24h
- 30 min — Outreach to one contractor or realtor for referral partnership
```

Rules:

- **No more than 5 recurring activities.** More = abandonment.
- **Each activity mapped to a channel and a leading metric.** No activity for activity's sake.
- **Fit the user's stated time.** Not close — fit. If it doesn't fit, cut activities.

## Business-type-specific metrics

### Local / trades
- **Google review velocity** — reviews/month (leading indicator for local SEO ranking, per Whitespark / BrightLocal data)
- **Call-to-quote ratio** — how many calls convert to quotes
- **Quote-to-job close rate** — how many quotes book
- **Repeat + referral %** — target 40%+ for mature trades
- **Cost per qualified lead** (not CAC — CAC is noisy at low volume)
- **Job margin** — not just revenue

### B2B SaaS / software
- **Activation rate** — % of signups who complete the first-value action
- **Retention cohorts** — week-4 and week-12 retention
- **LTV:CAC ratio** — target 3:1+ (Reforge benchmark)
- **Time-to-value** — median time from signup to first value moment

### Consulting / services
- **Qualified discovery calls / month**
- **Discovery-to-proposal rate**
- **Proposal-to-close rate**
- **Average engagement value**
- **Repeat engagement %**

### Prosumer / creator
- **Email subscribers (active, not total)**
- **Open rate** (30%+ = healthy)
- **Paid conversion rate**
- **Revenue per subscriber**

## Minimum-viable measurement stack

By business type:

| Type | Stack |
|---|---|
| **SaaS / dev tool** | Plausible or GA4 + PostHog (product analytics) + Stripe + spreadsheet |
| **Consulting** | CRM (Folk / Attio / Notion) + calendar + invoicing tool + spreadsheet |
| **Local trade** | Google Business Profile insights + call tracking (CallRail) + spreadsheet + reviews tool (NiceJob) |
| **Services** | Booking tool (Cal.com / Calendly) + CRM + spreadsheet |
| **Creator** | Newsletter tool (ConvertKit / Beehiiv) analytics + spreadsheet |

The spreadsheet is non-negotiable. One tab per leading indicator, one row per week.

## Risks and countermeasures

List 2–3 concrete risks. Not vague ("what if it doesn't work") — specific:

- "If Houzz is saturated, month-2 inbound could stall. **Countermeasure:** have channel 2 (contractor partnerships) already in progress by end of cycle 1, even if slower-growing."
- "If user's weekly time shrinks below 3 hours, content cadence breaks first. **Fallback:** cut content to bi-weekly, preserve response time and review-solicitation."

Each risk has a **trigger signal** (what the user would observe) and a **countermeasure** (what they'd do). No "we'll deal with it."

## Tomorrow morning

Close the plan with **one concrete action** the user will do in the next 24 hours. Not "start Monday" — something they can do tonight or tomorrow morning.

If the strategy doesn't survive contact with tomorrow morning, it won't survive month 2.

## Anti-patterns

- **Vanity metrics** — followers, impressions, traffic without downstream action
- **>5 tracked metrics** — attention collapses
- **Metrics not tied to a weekly decision** — reporting without steering
- **Activity disguised as outcome** — "posts shipped" ≠ "signups from posts"
- **Aggregating across channels** — hides failing ones
- **No defined trigger for revisiting the plan** — set the 6-week cycle boundary as non-negotiable
