# Astemo discovery call — blueprint AI

First meeting with Hitachi Astemo (automotive parts — shock absorbers, engine components). I was the technical person exploring engagement options.

## Key takeaways

**They're already set on horizontal AI.** They have a consultant firm helping them integrate agentic solutions, and they shot down our questions about context engineering and governance — that layer is spoken for. Governance is probably still weak (adoption left to engineer discretion), but it's not where they want help from us.

**The door is blueprint/CAD reading.** Their crucial pain point: they still read blueprints manually, including paper ones (old blueprints especially), and want to speed this up with AI. They have CAD tools but no AI in that workflow. They asked us to demo previous work on 3D, 2D blueprints, and CAD reading using AI.

**Fit.** If we position as automotive/shock-absorber specialists, it matches their domain. We have some technical drawing projects but couldn't show them in this call.

**Their priority order.** Blueprints first. Other AI areas are covered by their existing consultant — not interested there right now.

**Next step implied but not set.** They want a demo of our blueprint/CAD AI capabilities. No concrete follow-up scheduled yet.
