# ESG boom — understanding why

Want to build deeper understanding of why ESG is booming right now, including the "why" questions beneath the surface.

## Reasons for the boom (initial thoughts)

1. **AI boom → ESG growth (biggest driver).** Higher electricity consumption from AI data centers drives demand for energy tracking, carbon offsets, and ESG reporting. A side effect that became a primary market force.

2. **Global warming (long tail).** Government investments exist but not the main driving force. The structural pressure is real but moves slower than market forces.

3. **Risk indicator for business.** Low ESG score = lost opportunities/capital. Partners may choose competitors with better ESG standing. Financial markets are baking ESG into risk assessment.

4. **Domino effect — protectionism via ESG.** Governments enforce ESG standards that favor internal manufacturers over external competitors. A trade policy tool dressed in sustainability language.

5. **Marketing & branding.** ESG scores used on stock exchanges and in B2B marketing. A signal to investors and partners that the company is forward-looking and responsible.

Need to keep digging into the deeper causal chains behind each of these.

## Key players in the ESG ecosystem

- **Certificate granters** — bodies that issue ESG certifications/ratings (e.g., S&P Global, MSCI, Sustainalytics, EcoVadis)
- **Platform providers** — companies like CDI that build the data infrastructure for tracking, reporting, and managing ESG metrics
- **Data consumers** — normal organizations trying to meet standards, report to regulators, or market their ESG standing
- **Governments** — setting regulations, enforcement standards, using ESG as trade policy tool

## ESG standards by region

### APAC
- **Japan:** TCFD-aligned (Task Force on Climate-Related Financial Disclosures), METI guidelines, GPIF (pension fund) pushing ESG integration
- **Korea:** K-ESG Guidelines (voluntary), Korea Exchange ESG disclosure mandate phased in
- **China:** Carbon neutrality by 2060, mandatory environmental disclosure for listed companies, pilot carbon markets
- **SEA:** Singapore — SGX sustainability reporting mandatory, MAS (Monetary Authority of Singapore) green finance push. Thailand — SET ESG ratings. Vietnam — emerging, no mandatory framework yet but ISO 14001 adoption growing.

### Europe
- **CSRD (Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive)** — phased in from 2024, broadest scope, covering all large companies and listed SMEs
- **ESRS (European Sustainability Reporting Standards)** — detailed reporting standards under CSRD
- **SFDR (Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation)** — for financial market participants
- **EU Taxonomy** — classification system for sustainable economic activities
- **CBAM (Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism)** — carbon tariff on imports

### Americas
- **US:** SEC climate disclosure rules (pending/rolling out), California climate laws (SB 253, 261 — broadest US state-level), voluntary frameworks widely used (SASB, GRI, TCFD)
- **Brazil:** B3 (stock exchange) sustainability reporting recommended, emerging regulation

### Australia
- **Australia:** AASB (Australian Accounting Standards Board) developing sustainability reporting standards aligned with ISSB. Mandatory climate-related financial disclosure for large entities from 2024–25. ASIC enforcement on greenwashing increasing.
