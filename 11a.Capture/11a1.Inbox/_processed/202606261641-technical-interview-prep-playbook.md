Technical interview prep playbook for technical roles in general:

1. Gather information:
   - Job description
   - Customer/company information
   - Notes from people in my company who have worked with them before

2. Throw everything into agents. First, ask clarifying questions to make sure all the context we have about the company and job is in sync.

3. Give the agent my information through documents and a grill session.

4. Ask the agent to define fit and unfit places inside the CV.

5. Tell the agent to suggest multiple ways to approach the interview. I only know the story-based approach; what else is available?
   - Story-based (STAR/CAR/PAR): use a concrete episode with situation, task, action, result, and lesson learned.
   - Principle-based: lead with my engineering philosophy, then prove it with a short example.
   - Decision-based: focus on tradeoffs, options considered, constraints, and why I chose one path.
   - Problem-solving narrative: frame the answer as an investigation with hypotheses, evidence, and solution.
   - Impact-first: start with the measurable outcome, then explain how I achieved it.
   - Ownership-based: clarify exactly what I owned, what decisions were mine, and what teammates owned.
   - Failure-and-learning: use a real mistake to show humility, self-awareness, correction, and growth.
   - Teaching approach: explain the work as if teaching another engineer, starting from the need instead of the tool.
   - Design review style: present the work through requirements, constraints, alternatives, architecture, risks, and future improvements.
   - Business-outcome approach: connect engineering decisions to user value, business value, risk, cost, or delivery speed.
   - Hybrid: combine impact, problem, decision, story, and reflection when one answer needs multiple signals.
   - Match the frame to the question: difficult bug → problem-solving narrative; architecture choice → decision-based; scalable system → design review; failure → failure-and-learning; debugging process → principle-based.

6. Tell the agent to give me the 5 highest-ROI parts I should prepare for, phrased as open questions related to my old projects.

7. Answer each question and tell the agent to rephrase it into a story with:
   - Hook/problem
   - The stack
   - How I solved it
   - Lesson learned

8. Tell the agent the sections I am not too familiar with and the unfit parts. Ask it to try to solve them with my current/last project tech, focusing on concepts rather than tech stack.

9. Create artifacts and tell the agent to tag them. Tags must be unique and at the start of the doc. List all the tags for me to remember.
