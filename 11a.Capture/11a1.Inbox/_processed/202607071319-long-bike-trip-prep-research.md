### 1. Clothing, accessories & training for 60–80km

**Clothing — the padded bib short is the one non-negotiable; everything else is comfort/performance.**

| Piece | Why it matters | Budget → Premium brands |
|---|---|---|
| Bib shorts (chamois pad) | Prevents saddle soreness, no waist seam pressure, stays put. Never wear underwear under it. | Decathlon Triban/Van Rysel → Pearl Izumi, Castelli, dhb → Rapha, Assos, MAAP, Le Col |
| Cycling jersey | Rear pockets for snacks/phone/tube; breathable; snug = no flapping. | Decathlon → Giordana, Pearl Izumi → Rapha, Assos, MAAP |
| Base layer (hot-weather mesh) | Wicks sweat, stops jersey clinging, adds cooling. Optional but nice in VN heat. | Decathlon → Craft, Pearl Izumi → Rapha |
| Gloves (fingerless, palm pad) | Damps road buzz, prevents numbness, protects palms in a fall. | Decathlon → Pearl Izumi, Giro → Rapha, Assos |
| Helmet | Non-negotiable; well-ventilated. MIPS = nice-to-have. | Decathlon → Specialized, Giro, Bell, Lazer → POC, ABUS |
| Shoes | Flats: Five Ten, Vans. Clipless (SPD/SPD-SL): more efficient for 60–80km but learning curve. | Shimano, Specialized, Giro, Fizik |
| Sunglasses | Wind/bugs/UV; clear lens for low light. | Tifosi → Oakley, POC, Rapha |
| Arm/leg sleeves | Removable UV protection + warmth management. | Most cycling brands |
| Wind gilet / packable rain jacket | Fits in jersey pocket; blocks wind on descents / rain. | Decathlon → Rapha, Gore (ShakeDry) |

For VN heat: light-colored, lightweight, breathable jersey; UV arm sleeves; sweatproof sport sunscreen. Sun protection matters on a 3–4 hour ride.

**Accessories & prep tips:**
- **Fit > gear.** A bad bike fit causes pain at 60–80km you'd never notice at 20km. Get a bike fit (saddle height, fore/aft, reach) at a local shop.
- **Saddle** must match sit-bone width — width matters more than padding; a firm correctly-shaped saddle beats a squishy one. Brands: Specialized (Romin/Power), ISM (noseless), Brooks (leather, breaks in).
- **Hydration:** two bottle cages, 500–750ml each; ~500ml/hr, more in heat. Electrolytes are critical in VN humidity to avoid cramping (Nuun, Scratch, Maurten, or salt + a little sugar in water). Start sipping early.
- **Nutrition:** 30–60g carbs/hr from the first hour (bananas, energy bars, gels — Gu, Maurten, SiS — or real food like rice cakes / PB sandwich). Skip it and you bonk (glycogen crash: suddenly empty, dizzy, legs of lead).
- **Lights:** flashing tail light even in daytime (Bontrager Flare, Lezyne); front for tunnels/late finish.
- **Chamois cream** (Assos, BodyGlide) — anti-chafe, big help if you sweat a lot.
- **Pace discipline:** ride easy (conversational effort), don't burn matches early.

**Training regimen — plan ~6–8 weeks from low fitness. The weekly long ride is the single most important session; it builds fat-burning, saddle tolerance, and mental endurance. You can't shortcut saddle time.**

Progression (long ride +10–15%/week):
- Wk 1–2: 2–3 rides/wk, 15–25km each, easy pace (can hold a conversation).
- Wk 3–4: add a long ride → 30–40km; others 20–25km.
- Wk 5–6: long ride 45–55km; add some hills.
- Wk 7–8: long ride 60–70km → ready for 60–80km.

Key principles:
- 80/20: most rides easy (Zone 2, conversational); one long ride/wk building distance; optionally one ride with harder efforts/intervals once you have a base.
- Consistency > hero days: 3–4 rides/week beats one big ride.
- The long ride is specific prep — it's what makes 60–80km feel normal.
- Core strength (planks, dead bugs) helps back comfort; light squats help legs; mobility/stretching.
- Recovery: sleep, easy days, protein. Don't ride hard daily.
- Train your gut: practice eating/drinking on long rides so fueling doesn't upset your stomach.

On the 60–80km day:
- Start easy, settle into Zone 2. Eat/drink from the start. Spin, don't mash — cadence ~80–90 rpm saves knees.
- Stand occasionally to relieve saddle pressure; stretch at stops every 30–45km.
- Break it mentally into segments (next town, next water stop).

Common failure modes → fix:
- Bonk → eat carbs during the ride.
- Cramp → electrolytes + hydration (esp. VN heat).
- Saddle soreness → correct saddle + bib + chamois cream + gradual buildup.
- Knee pain → saddle height (too low = knee pain), higher cadence.
- Back/neck pain → fit (reach too long) + core strength.

**Where to get gear in Vietnam:**
- Decathlon (HCMC/Hanoi) — budget starting point: bikes, bibs, jerseys, helmets, tools, bottles, lights, pumps. Excellent price-to-function.
- Local bike shops (HCMC Q1/Q7/Thu Duc, Hanoi) — Specialized, Trek, Giant, Merida, Pinarello; gear + bike fits + service.
- Shopee/Lazada — cheap accessories (bottles, lights, tools); quality varies, read reviews.
- Premium gear (Rapha, Assos, Castelli, MAAP) — limited locally; order internationally or via proxy/freight forwarder.
- Facebook cycling groups ("Cycling Vietnam" etc.) — used gear, group rides, local route knowledge. Great community resource.
