# Presale-to-Delivery Handoff

The transition from presale (selling) to delivery (executing) is the highest-risk handoff in ITO. Misaligned expectations here destroy margins and client relationships.

## Why Handoffs Fail

| Failure Mode | Cause | Consequence |
|--------------|-------|-------------|
| **Scope creep** | Presale promised features not in contract | Unpaid development work |
| **Timeline shock** | Delivery learns deadline is impossible | Team burnout, client disappointment |
| **Quality mismatch** | Different definitions of "done" | Rejection at acceptance |
| **Team mismatch** | A-team presented, B-team delivered | Client trust breakdown |
| **SLA confusion** | Unstated response time expectations | Escalations, contract disputes |

## The Handoff Checklist

### Contract & Commercial
- [ ] Signed SOW with clear scope boundaries
- [ ] Pricing model documented (fixed, T&M, monthly rate)
- [ ] Payment terms and invoicing schedule
- [ ] Change order process defined
- [ ] Liability caps and insurance verified

### Technical Requirements
- [ ] Functional requirements documented and reviewed
- [ ] Non-functional requirements (performance, security, scale) captured
- [ ] Technology stack agreed and documented
- [ ] Integration points and dependencies mapped
- [ ] Definition of Done defined and shared

### Delivery Model
- [ ] Team composition and seniority levels confirmed
- [ ] Onboarding plan and timeline
- [ ] Communication cadence (standups, demos, reports)
- [ ] Escalation path defined (delivery → presale → executive)
- [ ] Timezone overlap and working hours confirmed

### Expectations & Assumptions
- [ ] Customer stakeholders and their roles documented
- [ ] Customer responsibilities (access, data, decisions) listed
- [ ] Known risks and mitigation plans captured
- [ ] Success criteria from the customer's perspective documented

## Handoff Meeting Structure

**Attendees**: Presale lead, Delivery lead, Account Manager, Key customer stakeholder (optional but recommended)

| Phase | Duration | Topics |
|-------|----------|--------|
| **Context** | 15 min | Why we won, what sold the deal, customer hot buttons |
| **Contract Review** | 20 min | Scope, timeline, pricing, key clauses |
| **Technical Deep Dive** | 30 min | Architecture decisions, constraints, known unknowns |
| **Risk & Issues** | 15 min | What's fragile, what needs monitoring, escalation triggers |
| **Q&A** | 10 min | Delivery team asks presale anything unclear |
| **Action Items** | 10 min | Who does what by when to close gaps |

## Ongoing Presale-Delivery Coordination

Handoff is not a single meeting — it's an ongoing relationship:

- **Presale stays involved** through first 30 days to answer questions and protect deal intent
- **Delivery feeds back** win/loss insights to improve future presale
- **Joint retrospectives** after project completion to improve handoff process

## Notes

- The presale team knows the customer's "why" — don't lose that knowledge at contract sign.
- Delivery teams often perceive presale as over-promising; presale perceives delivery as under-delivering. Structured handoff reduces this friction.
- Document assumptions explicitly — the things "everyone knows" are the things that cause disputes later.
