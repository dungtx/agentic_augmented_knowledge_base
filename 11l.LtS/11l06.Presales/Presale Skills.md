# Presale Skills

Capabilities required to execute and scale presale functions in an ITO company.

## Core Competencies

| Skill | Description | How to Develop |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **Technical Communication** | Translate complex technical concepts for business audiences | Practice elevator pitches; shadow sales calls; get feedback on clarity |
| **Business Case Development** | Build ROI models and cost-benefit analyses | Learn customer's P&L; study finance basics; use templates |
| **Competitive Positioning** | Differentiate against other ITO vendors | Win/loss reviews; competitive intel; positioning frameworks |
| **Objection Handling** | Address concerns without being defensive | Prepare scripts for common objections; role-play; learn reframing |
| **Negotiation** | Protect margin while winning deals | Training (Harvard/FAWS negotiation courses); practice with procurement |
| **Cross-functional Coordination** | Align sales, delivery, legal, finance | Map stakeholders; establish handoff protocols; build relationships |

## Management Skills for Scaling

As presale teams grow, individual contributors must become leaders:

### Delegation & Prioritization
- **Challenge**: Too many deals, not enough senior presales
- **Solution**: Clear qualification criteria; assign junior presales to qualified deals; protect senior time for complex opportunities

### Hiring & Onboarding
- **Challenge**: New hires take 3-6 months to be productive
- **Solution**: Structured onboarding with shadowing → co-pitching → solo with review → independent

### Quality Assurance
- **Challenge**: Inconsistent proposal quality as team scales
- **Solution**: Review checkpoints; proposal templates; win/loss analysis; peer review

### Knowledge Management
- **Challenge**: Knowledge trapped in individual heads
- **Solution**: Playbooks for common deal types; reusable proposal sections; case study library; objection handbook

## Common Objections in ITO Presales

Prepare responses for:

| Objection | Reframe |
|-----------|---------|
| "Your rates are too high" | "Let's look at total cost of ownership and risk-adjusted value" |
| "Offshore quality is poor" | "Here's our quality process, metrics, and client references" |
| "Timezone will be a problem" | "Here's our overlap model and async communication practices" |
| "We had a bad experience with outsourcing" | "Let's diagnose what went wrong and how we'd prevent it" |
| "Your team is too small/unknown" | "Here's our growth trajectory, client logos, and stability assurance" |

## Notes

- Scaling presale headcount without management discipline creates inconsistent win rates.
- Invest in templates, playbooks, and coaching before adding bodies.
- The best presales people are translators — between technical and business, between customer needs and internal capability.
