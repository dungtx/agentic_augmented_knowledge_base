# Deal Qualification

Determining whether a sales opportunity is worth investing presale effort into. Qualification happens after [[Lead Generation]] and before significant presale investment.

## Why Qualification Matters

- Presale effort is expensive (proposals, demos, RFP responses)
- Not every lead is a good fit
- Early disqualification saves time for winnable deals
- Qualification criteria force sales-presale alignment

## Qualification Frameworks

### BANT (Classic)

| Criteria | Questions to Ask |
|----------|------------------|
| **Budget** | Is there allocated budget? What's the range? |
| **Authority** | Who can sign? Who influences? Who can block? |
| **Need** | Is the pain acute? Is there a compelling event? |
| **Timeline** | When does the decision need to be made? |

### MEDDIC (Enterprise/Complex Deals)

| Criteria | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **Metrics** | Quantifiable business impact the customer seeks |
| **Economic Buyer** | Person with budget authority |
| **Decision Criteria** | Formal and informal factors for vendor selection |
| **Decision Process** | Steps, stakeholders, timeline to contract |
| **Identify Pain** | Specific problems driving the purchase |
| **Champion** | Internal sponsor who advocates for you |

## ITO-Specific Qualification Factors

| Factor | Red Flag | Green Flag |
|--------|----------|------------|
| **Deal Size** | Below minimum viable engagement | Clear budget aligned with pricing model |
| **Engagement Model** | Unclear scope, shifting requirements | Defined service model (staff aug, project, managed) |
| **Procurement** | No procurement process, or extremely rigid | Reasonable RFP process with clear evaluation criteria |
| **Incumbent** | Strong incumbent with renewal advantage | Incumbent underperforming or contract ending |
| **Technical Fit** | Technology stack completely outside expertise | Stack overlaps with proven capability |
| **Geographic/Timezone** | Requires timezone coverage you can't provide | Overlap hours match your delivery model |
| **Compliance** | Requirements you can't meet (e.g., clearance, certifications) | Certifications in place or achievable |

## Qualification Decision

```
Lead → Qualify → [Pass] → Invest in Presale Validation
              → [Fail] → Disqualify early (nurture or drop)
```

### When to Walk Away

- No budget or budget completely misaligned
- Decision process opaque or already decided (incumbent favored)
- Unrealistic timeline or scope for the budget
- Cultural/ethical misalignment with customer
- Compliance requirements you cannot meet
- Geographic/timezone requirements outside your capability

## Notes

- Qualification is not static — re-qualify at each stage as new information emerges.
- In ITO, "just exploring" leads often consume significant presale effort without closing. Set boundaries early.
- A qualified "no" is better than an unqualified "maybe" — protect your team's capacity for winnable deals.
