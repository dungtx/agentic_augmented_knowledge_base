# Service Offering (SO)

How to articulate and align the company's services with customer needs.

## Key Activities

- **Understand our Service Offering (SO)** — Internal clarity on what we sell
- **Customer discovery / analysis** — Deep research into the customer's business
- **Understand pain points** — Identify specific problems the customer faces
- **Describe our SO to sales and customers** — Enable consistent messaging

## ITO Service Models

| Model | Description | Best For |
|-------|-------------|----------|
| **Staff Augmentation** | Provide individual developers to customer teams | Filling skill gaps, scaling headcount quickly |
| **Project-Based** | Deliver defined scope on fixed or T&M contract | Greenfield development, discrete deliverables |
| **Managed Team** | Dedicated team with embedded management | Ongoing product development, long-term partnership |
| **Managed Service** | Full responsibility for function/outcome | QA, DevOps, 24/7 support with SLA |

## Discovery Focus Areas

- Current technology stack and gaps
- Team capacity and skill shortages
- **Timezone and communication preferences** — critical for offshore/nearshore ITO
- **Compliance requirements** — ISO 27001, SOC 2, GDPR, HIPAA (often hard requirements)
- Budget cycles and procurement constraints
- Existing vendor relationships and switching costs

## Notes

- Presales must act as a translator between technical capability and business problem.
- Sales teams need simplified, repeatable descriptions of SO to pitch effectively.
- In ITO, the service model (staff aug vs project vs managed) often matters more to the customer than the specific technology stack.
