# Value Proposition

What to impress the customer with during presale engagements. Frame your pitch around the questions your buyer is actually asking.

## The Buyer's Questions → Your Answers

| Buyer's Question | Your Pillar | How to Answer |
|-----------------|-------------|---------------|
| **"What do you do?"** | **Capability** | Clear, concise explanation of your [[Service Offering]] with relevant case studies and proof points |
| **"Why should I care?"** | **Fit** | Map specific capabilities to the customer's stated problems. Show fit, not just capability. |
| **"Why should I trust you?"** | **Assurance** | Delivery guarantees, SLAs, risk-mitigation mechanisms, references, certifications, security posture |

## ITO-Specific Value Levers

| Lever | How to Articulate |
|-------|-------------------|
| **Cost Efficiency** | Not just "cheaper" — show equivalent output at X% lower total cost of ownership |
| **Speed to Scale** | "Team in place within 2 weeks" vs 3-month local hiring cycle |
| **Timezone Coverage** | Follow-the-sun development, 24/7 support coverage |
| **Risk Mitigation** | ISO 27001, SOC 2, established delivery methodology, escrow arrangements |
| **Quality Assurance** | Code review practices, automated testing, SLA-backed quality metrics |
| **Flexibility** | Scale up/down without employment liability, try-before-commit models |

## Common Mistakes

- Leading with capability (Pillar 1) without establishing fit (Pillar 2) — customers tune out
- Claiming "high quality" without proof — every vendor says this
- Ignoring assurance (Pillar 3) — in ITO, trust is the buying blocker, not capability

## Notes

- The second pillar (fit vs pain point) is the differentiator; most vendors stop at describing SO.
- Assurance reduces perceived risk, which is often the real buying blocker in ITO.
- Match the value lever to the persona: Economic Buyer → cost/ROI; Technical Buyer → quality/process; User Buyer → flexibility/collaboration.
