# Sales Pipeline

The sequence of stages a prospect moves through from first contact to ongoing relationship.

## Stages

1. **Lead Generation** — Identify and attract potential customers ([[Lead Generation]])
2. **Qualification** — Assess if the opportunity is worth pursuing
3. **Pitching** — Initial outreach and value proposition
4. **Engagement** — Active dialogue and interest building
5. **Discovery & Consulting** — Needs assessment and solution shaping
6. **Demo** — Product or capability demonstration
7. **Presale Validation** — Pre-contract technical and commercial validation (formal RFI/RFP/Proposal process — see [[Presale Steps]])
8. **Closing** — Contract finalization and sign-off
9. **Development** — Project execution after contract sign
10. **Delivery** — Handover and go-live
11. **Upsale** — Expanding scope or selling additional services

## Key Transitions

| From | To | Critical Handoff |
|------|-----|------------------|
| Lead Generation | Qualification | Lead scoring criteria (budget, authority, need, timeline) |
| Presale Validation | Closing | Approved proposal, negotiated terms, risk sign-off |
| Closing | Development | Contract, SOW, technical requirements, expectations |
| Delivery | Upsale | Proven value, referenceability, expansion opportunity identified |

## Notes

- **Presale Validation** is the formal process of RFI → RFP → Proposal → RFQ/WBS → Contract negotiation. See [[Presale Steps]] for detail.
- **Closing** must happen *before* Development starts — in ITO, starting work without a signed contract is a common source of unpaid scope creep.
- The line between Presale Validation and Delivery is critical: presale sets expectations (timeline, scope, quality) that delivery must meet.
- Upsale depends heavily on delivery quality — delivered projects are your best sales collateral.
