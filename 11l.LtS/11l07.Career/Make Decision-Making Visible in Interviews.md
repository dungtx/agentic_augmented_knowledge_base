# Make Decision-Making Visible in Interviews

The strongest technical interview answers reveal a consistent way of thinking, not just a sequence of events.

Interviewers use stories, design prompts, and behavioral questions to infer whether my decisions are repeatable under new constraints. An answer is stronger when it exposes:

- what I noticed,
- what options I considered,
- what constraints mattered,
- what evidence changed my mind,
- what tradeoff I accepted,
- what I learned for next time.

Different answer frames make different parts of this thinking visible. Choose the frame that best matches the question.
