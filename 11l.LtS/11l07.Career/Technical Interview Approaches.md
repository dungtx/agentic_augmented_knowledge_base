# Technical Interview Approaches

Different interview questions ask for different signals. Pick the answer frame that makes the most useful signal visible.

## Approaches

- [[Story-Based Interview Answers]] — prove behavior through a concrete episode.
- [[Principle-Based Interview Answers]] — reveal repeatable engineering judgment.
- [[Decision-Based Interview Answers]] — show tradeoff thinking.
- [[Problem-Solving Narrative Interview Answers]] — show investigation and evidence.
- [[Impact-First Interview Answers]] — make the result memorable before explaining.
- [[Ownership-Based Interview Answers]] — clarify what was personally owned.
- [[Failure-and-Learning Interview Answers]] — show humility and growth.
- [[Teaching Approach Interview Answers]] — communicate technical work clearly.
- [[Design Review Style Interview Answers]] — present architecture like an engineering review.
- [[Business-Outcome Interview Answers]] — connect engineering to user or business value.
- [[Hybrid Interview Answers]] — combine frames when one answer needs multiple signals.

## Question fit

| Question | Strong frame |
|---|---|
| Tell me about yourself | Story + Impact |
| Describe a difficult bug | Problem-solving narrative |
| Why did you choose that architecture? | Decision-based |
| Tell me about a conflict | Story + Reflection |
| Design a scalable system | Design review style |
| Biggest accomplishment | Impact-first |
| How do you debug production issues? | Principle-based |
| Describe a failure | Failure-and-learning |
